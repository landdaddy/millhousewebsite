Coolidge, Arizona

Building in Coolidge.

Coolidge is home. Millhouse Built is based here, and Hunter and Jayla Kelley have been watching this town change — and watching the opportunity in it grow — for years. Coolidge has good bones: affordable land, a real community character, and a location that places it squarely in the middle of one of the most significant growth corridors in the state. The Sun Corridor, stretching between Phoenix and Tucson, runs right through this part of Pinal County, and Coolidge is positioned to benefit from that growth in ways that are just beginning to show.

For people who have been priced out of the Phoenix metro or who simply want more land for their money without sacrificing proximity to the city, Coolidge is a genuinely compelling place to build. You can find larger parcels here than almost anywhere else within a reasonable drive of the metro. The land is affordable, the permitting process is one we know inside and out, and the community is the kind that people put down roots in rather than use as a stepping stone. People who build custom homes here tend to intend to stay.

Building in your own backyard carries a certain weight, and we do not take it lightly. Every Millhouse project gets the same level of attention, but there is something particular about building in the community where we live and work. We know the neighbors, the local contractors, the character of different streets and neighborhoods, and what makes a Coolidge property sing. That local knowledge translates into practical advantages at every stage — from identifying the right land to navigating utilities to understanding which design choices fit the landscape and climate of this specific place.

Jayla Kelley leads design on every project, with an eye for materials and spatial decisions that feel rooted in how people actually live, not how a model home thinks they might. Hunter Kelley runs construction with the same care. The process is collaborative and unhurried — we stay small enough that every client matters, and every build gets built right.
