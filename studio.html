Our design process

What we do

Listen. Edit. Materialize.

Millhouse Studio is the design side of the build. We work with you closely, learn the way you want a space to feel, and shape that vision into something fitting, beautiful, and durable for the home.

Sometimes that means a paint palette and a few brave material choices. Sometimes it means showroom visits, stone slabs, wood tones, plumbing finishes, and the thousand quiet decisions that need one clear point of view.

Materials with memory

Showrooms, samples, and the good kind of second guessing.

We like the part where the house becomes touchable. The paint gets warmer. The wood gets softer. The stone slab has a little movement. The room starts to tell us what belongs.

Stone slab visits Paint and plaster tones Wood, tile, and hardware Furniture and finish direction

Led by Jayla Kelley

A sharp eye, a warm read on people, and really good taste.

Jayla brings a marketing background to the studio, which means she is naturally tuned to story, feeling, and how details make people respond. Her strength is reading the client, the house, and the moment, then helping the design become clearer without losing its soul.

She leads with conversation first. What do you keep saving? What feels too polished? What reminds you of home? From there, she helps edit the noise into a palette that feels personal, grounded, and beautiful to live with.

Short and sweet

The studio process.

Enough structure to make the decisions feel calm. Enough openness to let the best ideas breathe.

01

Gather

We learn your vision, the way you live, the references you love, and the feeling you want the house to hold.

02

Tailor

We narrow the palette, shape the material story, and make sure the design fits the space instead of fighting it.

03

Visit

We walk showrooms, look at slabs, compare samples, and make the big choices feel less abstract.

04

Settle

We bring the decisions into the build so the final home feels layered, useful, and quietly artful.
