The Case for Quiet Stripes

A stripe is one of the easiest ways to give a room a pulse without making it louder. It is structure with a soft voice.

Try them in places that already want order: benches, breakfast nooks, utility rooms, lampshades, bed skirts, powder rooms, and long hallways.

The best stripes do not ask the room to become about pattern. They simply give the eye somewhere steady to land.
