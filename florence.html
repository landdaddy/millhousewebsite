Florence, Arizona

Building in Florence.

Florence is the seat of Pinal County, and it carries the kind of history that newer Arizona towns are still working to earn. The historic downtown, the old courthouse, the long streets with mature trees — there is a character here that draws a particular kind of person. The people who build custom homes in Florence tend to want roots. They are not looking to flip in five years. They want a home that fits the place and fits the life they are building here, and they are willing to take the time to do it right.

Millhouse Built knows Pinal County the way you know a place you have worked in for years. Our team is based in Coolidge, just a few miles away, and we have navigated permits, parcels, utilities, and contractors across this county many times over. That familiarity matters. It means we are not learning the local process on your dime. It means we know the land, the soil conditions, the quirks of different areas of town, and the people who make projects move. When you hire a builder from somewhere else, you are paying for that learning curve. When you work with Millhouse, that knowledge is already in the room.

Florence has good land availability and relatively affordable parcels compared to the Phoenix metro. For buyers who want space without the density that comes with suburban growth, the area offers real options. Larger lots, a quieter pace, and a community that is growing steadily rather than being overrun. The growth here is happening in a way that still leaves room for considered custom building — you are not chasing the last lot before the next subdivision breaks ground.

The Millhouse process keeps land acquisition, design, and construction in one conversation. Jayla Kelley leads the design work — interiors, materials, how the home is laid out to live in — and Hunter Kelley manages the build. The two stay in dialogue so that the home you imagine is the home that gets built, not a value-engineered version of it. We stay small enough to give every project real attention, and we take on work where we believe the fit is right for both sides.
